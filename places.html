FEZ

Fez is a historic city in northern Morocco, known for being one of the country’s most culturally rich and traditional places. It’s located northeast of the Atlas Mountains, about a few hours inland from cities like Rabat and Casablanca. Fez is famous for its ancient medina—one of the largest in the world—where you’ll find narrow alleys, old mosques, and bustling souks that feel like stepping back in time. It’s often seen as the spiritual and intellectual heart of Morocco, with a deep connection to Islamic history, education, and artisan traditions.

MARRAKESH

Marrakesh is a vibrant city in western Morocco, sitting just at the foot of the Atlas Mountains. It’s one of the country’s most iconic destinations, known for its lively atmosphere, colorful souks, and historic landmarks. The city blends tradition and modernity—offering everything from centuries-old palaces and gardens to trendy cafes and luxury riads. At the heart of it all is Jemaa el-Fnaa, the famous square where street performers, food stalls, and local life come together in a truly unforgettable experience.

RABAT

Rabat is the capital city of Morocco, located along the Atlantic coast and nestled at the mouth of the Bouregreg River. Unlike the hustle of Marrakesh or Fez, Rabat has a more laid-back and modern vibe while still holding onto its historical charm. It’s home to important government buildings, embassies, and royal palaces, as well as historic sites like the Hassan Tower and the Kasbah of the Udayas. With its clean streets, coastal views, and a mix of old and new, Rabat offers a refreshing look at Morocco’s evolving identity.

CASABLANCA

Casablanca is Morocco’s largest city and a major economic hub, located on the country’s Atlantic coast. It’s a modern, fast-paced metropolis known for its business districts, contemporary lifestyle, and iconic landmarks. One of its most famous attractions is the Hassan II Mosque—one of the largest mosques in the world, perched right by the ocean. While it’s more modern than traditional cities like Fez or Marrakesh, Casablanca still offers glimpses of classic Moroccan charm, especially in its architecture, markets, and coastal cafés.

CHEFCHAOUEN

Chefchaouen is a small city tucked away in the Rif Mountains of northern Morocco, best known for its stunning blue-washed buildings and peaceful mountain vibes. Often called the “Blue Pearl” of Morocco, it’s a favorite for travelers looking to slow down and take in the beauty of its narrow streets, cozy cafés, and scenic views. Beyond its unique look, Chefchaouen offers a blend of Andalusian and Moroccan culture, local handicrafts, and a calm, welcoming atmosphere that feels worlds away from the busier cities.
